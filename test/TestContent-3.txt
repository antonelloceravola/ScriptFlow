*Title: Emotional Ignorance

:Why We Feel Everything and Understand Almost Nothing

From the moment we wake up, go to work, buy coffee, or meet friends, we experience countless emotions till we go to sleep, but rarely truly invest the time to understand them or use them to improve our life.

*Preface

-Antonello

As a child, I was always curious about people and their behavior. Yet, for reasons I could not fully understand at that time, their behavior often felt difficult for me to interpret. I was one of those children who could easily spend hours absorbed in playing, building, or exploring an idea, and regularly paying too little attention to the subtle signals others were exchanging around me. That distance created a barrier.

In many ways, that question became one of the seeds of this book.

*Introduction: Breaking the Mask

We have all heard the idea that everyone wears a mask in public. A familiar story, repeated many times. The idea that we present a public version of ourselves to the world — more controlled, more acceptable, more protected — while keeping our fragile, uncertain, or vulnerable parts hidden inside.

This book looks at a different kind of mask. A mask can not be taken off.
Not one we put on in front of others, but one we slowly grow over time. A mask made of habits, expectations, and learned reactions. Layer after layer, it forms in response to family, school, work, and society — not to deceive, but to function. And forgetting it is the beginning of our emotional ignorance.

And that is the problem.

-The Mask We Grow

When we are born, we are direct. Not naïve. Not empty. Just direct. We cry when something hurts. We smile when something feels good. We reach out when we want closeness, and we pull away when we don’t. There is no strategy in it. No performance. No interpretation. What happens inside us appears immediately on the outside.
The mask grows thicker.

Because without access to what happens inside us, we have no real way to explain what we do. And without understanding what we do, change remains accidental.
